JAN 29

US Airforce Crash

  • A US Army helicopter crashed into a commercial jet during night training near Washington D.C., killing all 67 people on board in one of the deadliest aviation disasters in recent history.
  • The Army Black Hawk helicopter was conducting routine night training exercises along the Potomac River when it collided with American Airlines Flight 5342 approaching Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport.
  • Investigations revealed that the helicopter's flight path and the landing path of commercial aircraft were never designed to ensure proper separation, and the dangers posed by these crisscrossing paths were never adequately reviewed by aviation authorities.
  • The tragedy sparked a nationwide review of military training protocols near civilian airports and led to calls for improved air traffic coordination systems.
